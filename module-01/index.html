The Blood Codex — Module One

This module introduces the foundational lens of The Blood Codex: menstruation not as waste, pathology, or inconvenience, but as a biological and symbolic information system.

Rather than prescribing belief, optimization, or healing outcomes, this module teaches literacy — how to read blood as data shaped by physiology, culture, power, and meaning.

What This Module Covers

  • Science — menstrual physiology, cycle timing, bodily signals
  • Theology & Myth — blood as covenant, taboo, offering, threshold
  • Philosophy — meaning, embodiment, power, recurrence
  • Psychology — projection, mirroring, somatic memory
  • Culture & Politics — regulation, shame, medicalization

The goal is not belief adoption, but pattern recognition — learning how menstruation has been framed, controlled, and misread across systems.

Practical Body Lab

  • Blood Tracking — color, texture, flow, mood
  • Product Audit — pads, tampons, cups, paper, chemical exposure
  • Safer Swaps — organic cotton, cups, unbleached materials
  • Self-Exam — cervical position, cycle mapping
  • Drainage — gentle abdominal massage, lymph flow

Integration Practice

  1. Keep a Blood Journal — physical, emotional, symbolic
  2. Create a Cycle Altar — salt, stone, candle, plant matter
  3. Perform an Earth Offering — first or last day of bleed
  4. Write reflection: What does my blood mean to me?

Blood is not an accident of biology.

It is covenant, portal, meaning, mirror, and data.

Your first task is to read it with all five voices alive —
the theologian, the mystic, the philosopher, the psychologist, the scientist —

and to weave them into your own authority.
